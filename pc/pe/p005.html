Smallest multiple

pe link

This problem requires no programming and be solved by hand, although for larger numbers, it can be implemented rather easily. Although $20!$ would certainly work, it over counts some values, for example, $20$ is just $4\cdot 5$ so as long as we have one factor of both, the answer will be divisible by 20. In full, the factors are

$$ \begin{array}{c|l} n & \text{Factors} \\ \hline 1 & 1 \\ 2 & 2 \\ 3 & 3 \\ 4 & 2^{2} \\ 5 & 5 \\ 6 & 2\cdot 3\\ 7 & 7\\ 8 & 2^{3} \\ 9 & 3^{2} \\ 10 & 2\cdot 5\\ 11 & 11\\ 12 & 2^{2}\cdot 3\\ 13 & 13\\ 14 & 2\cdot 7\\ 15 & 3\cdot 5\\ 16 & 2^{4} \\ 17 & 17 \\ 18 & 2\cdot 3^{2}\\ 19 & 19\\ 20 & 2^{2} \cdot 5 \end{array} $$

To get the answer, we need the largest power of each prime that is smaller than the maximum value. Effectively, this means that for each prime, its largest power is

$$e_{i} = \bigg\lfloor{\frac{\log_{10}(i)}{\log_{10}(N)}\bigg\rfloor}, $$

and the answer is

$$\prod_{p\le N} p_{i}^{e_{i}}.$$

Thus the answer is $2^{4}\cdot 3^{2} \cdot 5 \cdot 7 \cdot 9 \cdot 11 \cdot 13 \cdot 17 \cdot 19 = 232792560$.
