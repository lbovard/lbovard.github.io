Multiples of 3 and 5

pe link

This problem is very simple using the inclusion and exclusion principle. We simply count all the multiples of 3, all the multiples of 5, and then subtract the multiples of 3 & 5. Translated into a formula, this becomes,

$$S = 3\sum_{i=1}^{\lfloor 999/3\rfloor} i+ 5\sum_{i=1}^{\lfloor 999/5\rfloor} i- 15 \sum_{i=1}^{\lfloor 999/15\rfloor} i, $$

which can further be simplified because

$$ \sum_{i=1}^{n}i = \frac{n(n+1)}{2}.$$

The solution is now easy to code.
