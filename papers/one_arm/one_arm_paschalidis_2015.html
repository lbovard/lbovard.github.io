One-arm Spiral Instability in Hypermassive Neutron Stars Formed by Dynamical-Capture Binary Neutron Star Mergers

2015 - Paschalidis, East, Pretorius, Shapiro

DOI, arXiv

In this paper the one-arm instability is studied for eccentric binary neutron star mergers. The study two neutron stars of mass $1.35 M_{\odot}$ with $J/M^{2} = 0.05$. The initial separation is $200$ km and an impact parameter of $r_{p}=8$.

The equation of state used is the HB piecewise polytropic cold equation of state. To incorporate thermal heating, a thermal component is added to the equation of state. No magnetic fields or radiation transport was used.

To study the instability, the following quantity is defined

$$C_{m} = \int \rho_{0}u^{0}\sqrt{-g}e^{im\phi}d^{3}x,$$

which differs slightly in that the intergral is take over all the star instead of just the xy-plane.
