The One-Armed Spiral Instability in Neutron Star Mergers and its Detectability in Gravitational Waves

2016 - Radice, Bernuzzi, Ott

DOI, arXiv

This short paper analyses the one-arm instability in binary neutron star mergers and the detectability of the GWs. They study two mergers with $M=1.35M_{\odot}$ using SLy (soft) and MS1b (stiff) polytropic equations of state. The initial separation was $50$ km.

They use a standard modal decomposition of the density in the $xy$-plane as

$$P_{m} = \int \rho W e^{-im\phi}\sqrt{\gamma}dxdy,$$

where $\rho$ is the rest-mass density, $W$ is the Lorentz factor, $\gamma$ is the determinant of the three-metric. However later, GWs are used to show unambiguously the results.

The evolution of the density modal decomposition for the MS1b and SLy EOS. All modes saturate after about $10$ ms.

As can be seen, for the MS1b the $m=1$ mode is the largest for later times after about $10$ ms. However for the SLy $m=2$ dominates. In order to measure how effective these modes are, the gravitational waves are required. Defining the luminosity and angular momentum as

$$L_{GW} = \frac{1}{16\pi } |\dot{h}_{lm}|^{2}, \dot{J}_{GW} = \frac{m}{16\pi}\Im(h_{lm}\dot{h}_{lm}^{*})$$

The evolution of gravitational wave luminosity.

As can be seen for the MS1b, the high resolution runs show that the $m=1$ dominates at later times. Also, this mode does not undergo any damping compared with the $m=2$ mode. Calculation shows that $J/(2\dot{J}_{GW})> 100$ s for $m=1$. Furthermore, the sensitivity curves from aLIGO compared with the peak of the $m=1$ instability and it was demonstrated that it is, in principle, possible to see this mode in the signal given a long observation and a nearby signal.
