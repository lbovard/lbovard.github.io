Radial oscillations of relativistic stars

DOI link

This paper does a simple analysis of the radial oscillations of a non-rotating neutron star.

To proceed they start with the standard spherical metric for a spherically symmetric star

$$ ds^{2} = -e^{2\nu}dt^{2} + \left(1-\frac{2m(r)}{r}\right)dr^{2} + r^{2}d\Omega^{2}$$

and a perfect fluid. Plugging in one obtains the TOV equations. From there a radial perturbation is done of the form

$$\delta r(r,t) = X(r)e^{i\omega t}$$

which when plugged into the equations (expanded out?? clarify!) yields a complicated differential equation for $X(r)$. It turns out that the equation can be transformed into a Sturm-Liouville problem which means that $\omega^{2}$ eigenvalues are real and distinct. Furthermore, they form an ordered sequence. Additionally, the eigenfunctions exist and form a basis.

The goal is to solve the complicated boundary value problem for the eigenvalues and eigenmodes and analyse the results. This is done in two ways: 1) the shooting method 2)finite differencing of the derivatives and solving the resulting matrix problem.

Coupled to this, they use a whole list of EOS, but most prominently the APR1 and a few polytropes $(n=0.5,0.8,1.0)$. They find that the major limitation of the numerical accurary is due to the interpolation of the EOS table which results in errors of about 3% when using different types of interpolation.
