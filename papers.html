Fluid Mechanics

Stratified Fluid Flow Theory and Turbulence
  • Self-similarity of strongly stratified inviscid flows ~ Billant, Chomaz (2001)
    An article that states that previous scaling arguments of the Boussinesq lead to incorrect conclusions about the formulation of stratified turbulence.
  • Dynamics of turbulence strongly influenced by buoyancy ~ Riley, deBruynKops (2003)
    review goes here
  • Fluid Motions in the Presence of Strong Stable Stratification ~ Riley, Lelong (2000)
    A comprehensive review of stratified turbulence and theory known up until that point. A good review but slightly out of date.
  • Recent Progress in Stratified Turbulence (from Ten Chapters in Turbulence 2013) ~ Riley, Lindborg (2013)
    A comprehensive modern review of stratified turbulence and theory up until 2012. An update of the Riley-Lelong review paper.
  • Potential enstrophy in stratified turbulence ~ Waite (2013)
    Discusses the important of potential enstrophy in stratified turbulence.
Vortex Instabilities
  • Universal Short-Wave Instability of Two-Dimensional Eddies in an Inviscid Fluid ~ Pierrehumbert (1986)
    An article about vortex instability in subject to 3d perturbations.
  • Three-dimensional stability of a vortex pair ~Billant, Brancher, Chomaz (1999)
    The viscous unstratified three dimensional stability of the Lamb-Chaplygin vortex pair.
  • Instability of vortex leapfrogging ~ D.J. Acheson (2000)
    A simple little simulation of vortex leap-frogging.
  • Onset of secondary instabilities on the zigzag instability in stratified fluids ~ Augier, Billant (2011)
    An article that rectifies some differences in the DNS simulations of the stratified flow theory.
  • Three-dimensional stability of a vertical columnar vortex pair in a stratified fluid ~ Billant, Chomaz (2000)
    A numerical study of the zigzag instability and comparison with experimental results.
  • Three-dimensional instability of strained vortices in a stably stratified fluid ~ Miyazaki, Fukumoto (1992)
    Build on the work of Pierrehumbert 1986 to stratified fluids.

Numerical Methods (fluids)
  • Krylov Methods for the Incompressible Navier-Stokes Equations~ Edwards, Tuckerman, Friesner, Sorensen (1992)
    A paper that goes into detail of how to use Krylov methods in incompressible Navier-Stokes flow.
Renormalisation Theory (specifically fluids)
  • Simple Viscous Flows: From Boundary Layers to Renormalisation group
    A nice review article on applications of RG methods to low Reynolds number flow. Also contains a very detailed history on deriving flow around a cylinder and a sphere and their comparisons with experiments.
  • The Renormalization Group: A Perturbation Method for the Graduate Curriculum
    A review article that claims that the RG should be taught in a graduate perturbation theory course. Contains a good introduction to the RG through a few worked out examples and also applies RG to a problem in wave propagation in a nonlinear media.

Financial Mathematics and Computation

uncategorised papers
  • Nine ways to implement the binomial method for option valuation in MATLAB ~ D. Higham (2002)
    A very readable article about how to implement the binomial method in MATLAB. It explains how to exploit vectorisation to make really fast code. Plus it elaborates on using binomial sums for European options which are comparable in speeds to directly solving the Black-Scholes equations.
  • Efficient monte carlo algorithm for pricing barrier options ~ KS Moon (2008)
    A very readable paper about efficient ways to evaluate barrier options using Brownian bridges.
  • The pricing of options and corporate liabilities ~ F. Black, M. Scholes (1973)
    The paper that started it all.
  • MAPLE and MATLAB for Stochastic Differential Equations in Finance ~ D. Higham, P. Kloeden (2001)
    A fun little paper on solving SDEs numerically and symbolically in MAPLE and MATLAB.
  • A Closed-Form Solution for Options with Stochastic Volatility with Applications to Bond and Currency Options ~ S. Heston (1993)
    Develops the Heston stochastic volatiltiy model and provides a semi-analytic solution for European options.
Fourier Methods in Finance
  • Option Valuation Using the Fast Fourier Transform ~ P. Carr, D. Madan (1999)
    Evaluating option values through the characteristic function using FFTs. Applied to variance gamma options.
  • Efficient Options Pricing Using the FFT ~ Y.K. Kwok, K.S. Leung, H. Y. Wong (2012)
    A modern review of using FFTs in option evaluation. Discusses extension to multiple assets, Bermuda options, and network trees.
