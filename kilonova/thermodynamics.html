Thermodynamics¶

Basic Thermodynamics¶

Consider the expansion of pressure, \(p(\rho,T)\), where \(\rho\) is the rest-mass density and \(T\) is the temperature. Expanding this out as differentials yields the following relationship between the differentials

\[dp = \frac{\partial p}{\partial T}\bigg|_{\rho}\delta T + \frac{\partial p}{\partial \rho}\bigg|_{T}\delta \rho.\]

By diving this expression by various differentials, such as radius, yields a differential expression for various identities involving the pressure.

Stefan-Boltzmann Law¶

The Stefan-Boltzmann law relates the temperature of a blackbody to the power radiated away. In astronomy instead of using power, the term luminosity is used instead. The law states

\[L = \sigma A T^{4},\]
