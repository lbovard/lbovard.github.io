Magnetic Fields¶

In this section are scaling relations related to magnetic fields.

Ohmic dissipation¶

The timescale of the Ohmic dissipation, \(\tau_{d}\) can be expressed as

\[\tau_d \simeq 5\times 10^{11}\,\, \left(\frac{\rho}{1~{\rm g\,cm^{-3}}}\right)^{0.1} \bigg(\frac{T}{1\,{\rm MeV}}\bigg)^{0.8} \left(\frac{\lambda_B}{1\,{\rm km}}\right)^2~{\rm s} ,\]

where \(\rho\) is the rest-mass density, \(T\) is the temperature, and \(\lambda_{B}\) is the characteristic length of the magnetic field.

Source: Harutyunyan et al. 2018

Hall effect¶

The timescale of the Hall effect, \(\tau_{B}\) can be expressed as

\[\tau_B \simeq 1.2\times 10^{2}\, \left(\frac{B}{10^{13} \rm{G}}\right)^{-1} \left(\frac{\rho}{1~{\rm g\, cm^{-3}}}\right) \left(\frac{\lambda_B}{1~{\rm km}}\right)^2~{\rm s}\,,\]

where \(\rho\) is the rest-mass density, \(\lambda_{B}\) is the characteristic length of the magnetic field, and \(B\) is the magnetic field. Source: Harutyunyan et al. 2018

Neutrinos¶

Mean free path¶

The length scale of the mean free path of neutrinos, \(\lambda_{\nu}\) can be expressed as

\[\lambda_{\nu} \sim 2.56\times 10^{14} \left(\frac{\rho}{1~{\rm g\, cm^{-3}}}\right)^{-1}\left(\frac{E_{\nu}}{1\, \rm{MeV}}\right)^{-2}\, \rm{km},\]

where \(\rho\) is the rest-mass density and \(E_{\nu}\) is the energy of the neutrinos.

Source: see derivation in section neutrinos.

Cooling¶

Neutrinos inside the neutron star are trapped (see mean free path length scale) and thus will diffuse out over a timescale \(t_{\rm{cool}}\) given as

\[t_{\rm cool} \sim 800 \left(\frac{M}{2.8 M_{\odot}} \right)\left(\frac{R}{10\, \rm{km}}\right)^{-1} \left(\frac{E_{\nu}}{1\, \rm{MeV}}\right)^{2}\, \rm{ms},\]

where \(M\) is the mass, \(R\) is the radius of the merger remanent and \(E_{\nu}\) is the energy of the neutrinos.

Source: Paschalidis et al. 2012
