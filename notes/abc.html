$$n = p_{1}^{\alpha_{1}}p_{2}^{\alpha_{2}}\ldots p_{n}^{\alpha_{n}}$$

the radical of $n$ is given as

$$rad(n) = p_{1}p_{2}\ldots p_{n},$$

in other words the radical of a given number is simply the product of the unique primes the number is composed of.
