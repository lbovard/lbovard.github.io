Coupon's Collector Problem

The coupon collector's problem is the following problem: suppose a company is offering $N$ coupons in a giveaway in a maga. The probability of recieving any coupon is equal, i.e. $p=1/N$. What is the expected number of coupons we must collect before we obtain all $N$ coupons?

We need to find the expected value of getting the $i$th coupon. Suppose, for now, that the probability of finding the $i$th coupon is $p_{i}$. What is the probability of finding the coupon on the $k$th attempt? It is clear that this is $(1-p_{i})^{k-1}p_{i}$, i.e. the first $k-1$ attempts all fail, and it succeeds on the $k$th. This is the geometric distribution. So to find the expected number, we need to compute

$$E(p) = \sum_{k=1}^{\infty}k(1-p_{i})^{k}p_{i} = \frac{1}{p_{i}}.$$
